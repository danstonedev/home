Pre-built clinical simulation scenarios for physical therapy education.

DevPT simLAB helps students practice clinical reasoning through objective-aligned cases that integrate 3D pain patterns, simulated EMR documentation, AI patient conversations, imaging interpretation, and specialty treatment decisions.

simLAB bridges the gap between knowing PT content and performing like a developing clinician. It's not a bundle of tools — it's a scenario-first learning platform where each module supports a specific part of a pre-built case tied to course objectives.

Every case runs the full clinical arc.

Not "pick the right answer." Students move through the work of a real encounter — the skills that lecture, textbook questions, and isolated lab practicals don't build — and each step produces something faculty can assess.

  1. Chart review
  2. Interview
  3. Screen
  4. Reason & decide
  5. Educate
  6. Document
  7. Reflect & revise

Start with scenarios, not tools.

Each pack is a pre-built clinical case. It ships with learning objectives, a CAPTE/NPTE alignment, a student artifact, and an assessment rubric — designed to drop into a course with minimal faculty setup. Customization can come later.

The modules inside each case.

Each simLAB module can stand alone, but the real value appears when modules combine into pre-built cases tied to course objectives. simPAIN is the lead wedge — pain is central across DPT education and connects naturally to documentation, the patient interview, imaging, and longitudinal reasoning.

simPAIN

3D pain-pattern learning for clinical reasoning.

The signature scenario component: students learn to read pain distribution, quality, and behavior on a 3D body and reason from the pattern. simPAIN adapts the concept behind PainMap for education-focused, objective-aligned scenarios — it supports pain-pattern reasoning and communication, and clinicians remain responsible for interpretation.

// clinical & research platform

PainMap: pain in three dimensions.

PainMap is the advanced clinical and research platform behind DevPT's pain mapping — kept separate from simLAB. simPAIN adapts the concept for the classroom; PainMap is built for clinical pain science. Pain is still mostly recorded as a single number from 0 to 10, a measure that only loosely tracks the experience and misses its quality, spread, and change over time.4

  • Whole-body, in 3D — pain placed on a real model, not a flat outline.
  • Quality, not just intensity — sharp, dull, burning, tingling, and how far it spreads.
  • Change over time — pain that migrates and evolves across days and weeks.
  • A measurement model, not a diagnostic device — it supports documentation and research; clinicians remain responsible for interpretation.

It began as a teaching idea and has become a serious instrument for clinical pain science. It's live and in use at pain3d.com.

The case for simulation

Across more than 600 studies, technology-enhanced simulation produced large gains in knowledge and skills — and moderate gains in patient outcomes.1

JAMA, 2011
In physical therapy

Replacing up to a quarter of clinical placement with simulation produced comparable student competency across the core domains of PT practice.2

Physical Therapy, 2016
Sharper reasoning

Virtual patients strengthen clinical reasoning — especially history-taking, data gathering, and management decisions.3

BMC Medical Education, 2022
