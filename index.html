Every pack produces work you can drop into your existing rubric — a SOAP note, a clinical decision, and a reflection — mapped to the reasoning, communication, safety, and documentation skills students struggle with most at first rotation. And this is why scenarios matter for your program: in PT education, replacing up to a quarter of clinical placement with simulation produced comparable student competency across the core domains of practice.2

One pack, end to end.

Take Red Flags in Acute Low Back Pain (pilot-ready). A student works the case through the full arc — and leaves a trail you grade:

  1. Chart review reads the EMR and notices the bladder-symptom note → case summary
  2. Interview asks the saddle-anesthesia and symptom-progression questions → interview record
  3. Screen & decide recognizes the red-flag cluster and chooses refer vs treat → clinical decision
  4. Document writes a defensible note carrying the safety reasoning → SOAP note
  5. Reflect revises the decision after feedback → reflection

Each artifact maps to the pack's rubric, so it slots into how you already grade.

// clinical & research platform

simPAIN is built on PainMap.

PainMap is a clinical and research instrument for mapping pain in three dimensions — quality and spread, not just a 0–10 number4 — in real use at pain3d.com. It's a measurement model, not a diagnostic device; clinicians remain responsible for interpretation. simPAIN adapts the concept for the classroom.
